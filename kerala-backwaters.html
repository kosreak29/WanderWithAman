Kerala Backwaters

When I think of Kerala, I do not immediately think of destinations.

I think of moments.

The gentle movement of houseboats across silent waters. The reflection of palm trees dancing on emerald backwaters. The scent of rain carried by the breeze. The simplicity of life unfolding at its own pace.

◆

Among all the places I hope to visit in India, Kerala feels different.

It feels like a destination designed to slow the soul down.

I imagine waking up early in the morning, stepping outside onto a wooden deck, and watching the first light spread across the water.

No traffic. No noise. No deadlines.

Only nature. Only peace.

◆

Travel often teaches us to move.

Kerala, I believe, teaches us to pause.

To appreciate silence. To notice beauty in ordinary moments. To understand that sometimes the most unforgettable adventures happen when nothing extraordinary is happening at all.

I hope to explore hidden villages, local culture, traditional cuisine, and the stories of people who call these waterways home.

◆

Because every destination has landmarks.

But every place also has a soul.

And that is what I wish to discover.

This chapter remains unwritten.

But someday, I will stand beside those backwaters, camera in hand, ready to begin another story.
