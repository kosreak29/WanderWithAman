Chapter 03: The River That Calls Back

Omkareshwar, Madhya Pradesh

Ever since I completed my journey to Mahakaleshwar, there has been another name that keeps returning to my thoughts—Omkareshwar.

Perhaps it is the sacred flow of the Narmada.

Perhaps it is the spiritual energy that has drawn pilgrims for centuries.

Or perhaps it is simply another call from the road that refuses to be ignored.

◆

I imagine arriving before sunrise, when the first rays of light gently touch the waters of the Narmada and the island-shaped Jyotirlinga awakens with the sound of temple bells. The air would carry a sense of calm unlike any city I have ever known.

Walking through the narrow streets leading to the temple, I can already imagine meeting fellow travellers, devotees, and local people whose stories are deeply connected to the river.

Omkareshwar is not just another place on my travel list.

It feels like a destination where time slows down.

A place where every step reminds you that life does not always have to be rushed.

◆

I want to sit quietly by the riverbank, watch the evening aarti, and simply listen—to the water, to the bells, and perhaps to my own thoughts.

Maybe this journey will not give me answers.

Maybe it will only teach me better questions.

And sometimes, that is enough.

◆

The road to Omkareshwar remains unwritten.

But I have a feeling that when the day finally arrives, it will become one of the most meaningful chapters of Wander With Aman.
